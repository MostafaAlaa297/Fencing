-Born: July 16, 1990 (age 29 years), Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands.

*Kim Jung-Hwan*

-Kim Jung-hwan is a South Korean fencer. He was number 1 in the world in 2016 in sabre.
-Born: September 2, 1983 (age 36 years), Seoul, South Korea.

*Áron Szilágyi*

-Áron Szilágyi is a Hungarian sabre fencer, team world champion in 2007, two-time Olympic champion in 2012 and 2016 and European champion in 2015.
-Born: January 14, 1990 (age 30 years), Budapest, Hungary.
-Height: 1.8 m.
-Weight: 80 kg.
-Spouse: Beáta Szántó (m. 2017).
-Education: Károli Gáspár University of the Reformed Church in Hungary (2012–2016).
-Olympic medals: Fencing at the 2012 Summer Olympics - Men's sabre, Individual.

Rules:

-In 2017 we saw a return to sanity in the 4 meters. -The basic idea is simple: If you can adapt the distance of your attack, you are in preparation.

-Be ready for the consequences.

-Referees should split 4 meter calls with a razor, otherwise the simultaneous grind game will never die.

-As usual, all calls on video are the final bout ref's call or video appeal call. Please do not tell us a call is wrong unless you were one of the bout referees in question. If you were the bout referee and you want to point something out, we'd love to hear from you!

-The march in sabre has changed significantly since the introduction of the current cutoff times in 2004. Gone are the days when the attack in sabre meant that you would advance on your opponent expecting to finish with their counterattack.

-Over the last 10 years, sabreurs and referees have developed a whole new class of marching attacks. These marches focus on surprising the defender with rapid tempo and distance changes, rather than finishing with the counterattack.
